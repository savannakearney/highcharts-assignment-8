Americans' View on Cybersecurity

Charts by Savanna Kearney

Cybersecurity is becoming increasingly important among Americans today, especially as technology--and our knowledge of it--continues to evolve. According to Pew Research Center, many Americans do not generally trust other people, despite the fact that many of them do not take the necessary precautions to protect their Internet passwords. There are plenty of steps people can take to safely save their passwords, and thus their important personal information. For example, the most popular way to save Internet passwords among the Americans who took this survey is either to memorize them in their heads or write them down on a piece of paper--neither of which is the safest and smartest method. Very few of the people surveyed utilized password management programs, such as Dashlane, Lastpass or Apple Keychain. With the various instances of cybersecurity hacking in the United States, it is more important than ever to use the best practices for protecting your personal information--and your livelhood.
